Filters

Filters do what the name implies- they filter out frequencies from a sound (and occasionally emphasize some). This can darken or brighten a sound dramatically.

There are three main types of filters we'll focus on today:

1. Lowpass - Allows frequencies below the cutoff frequency to pass (and fiters out those above the cutoff frequency)
2. Hipass - Allows frequencies above the cutoff frequency to pass (and fiters out those below the cutoff frequency)
3. Bandpass - Allows frequencies within a narrow band to pass (and filters out those above and below that band)

If it sounds confusing, it will become clear as soon as you hear it.

Let's talk about Q- it's a little bit tricky. The Q adds a small boost in frequency at the frequency cutoff, right before the filter begins taking frequencies out. This has the effect of emphasizing the cutoff frequency. In practice, I find myself most often using it to achieve some iconic EDM bass sounds, with the filter set to Lowpass, a low cutoff frequency, and some Q. The higher the Q value, the more pronounced this little bump boost is.

In a bandpass filter, Q effects how narrow the frequency band that's allowed through is.

Play with the Q now and do some experimenting. The best way to learn these things is to hear them in action.
