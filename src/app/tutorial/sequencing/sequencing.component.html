Step Sequencing

Congrats, this is the last tutorial you need to know how to use all the parts of this app. We'll focus on the step sequencers in the instruments.

Step sequencers allow you to specify which notes should be played at which rhythmic place. Think of them as having an x and y axis. On the x axis is time. Each cell is a 16th note, and the 1/4 notes (and measures in this app) are highlighted with a different color. If you enabled every 4 cells, starting with the first, you'd get four notes on the beat. Every 2 cells would be eight eighth notes, etc.

The y axis comprises the different notes that are possible to sequence. On the C row, enable the first and 9th cells and on the G row enable the 5th and 13th, and you'll get 1/4 notes alternating between C and G.

Try the patterns I just mentioned on the sequencer below and it'll start to make more sense. The pattern you see represents 1 measure. The highlights on each 1/4 note will serve as a guide.

A drum machine sequencer works basically the same way. The only difference is the y axis. Instead of notes, we get different drums that we can trigger at different rhythmic values.

On the drum machine below, sequence the following pattern: The bass drum on all 4 quarter notes; The snare drum on the second and fourth quarter notes; The closed hat on all 4 beats; The open hat on the eighth notes in between quarters. If you can do that, you definitely understand step sequencing. Also you've made the basic disco techno beat.

Feel free to play around with these until you feel like you've got a decent grasp on the concept. Other than that, go make some music!
